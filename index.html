AuriStream-MTP: Unifying Speech Perception, Prediction and Generation with Multi-Token Prediction

Anonymous Sumbission

Humans learn language directly from speech, seamlessly integrating perception, prediction, and generation. In contrast, existing AI systems treat these as separate stages: speech representation models encode audio, and speech language models generate sequences over fixed discrete units, with no interaction between stages. We introduce AuriStream-MTP, a unified speech model that learns perception, prediction, and generation under a single objective: Multi-Token sound Prediction. Operating on fine-grained 5 ms acoustic tokens, it predicts 40 future steps (5â€“200 ms) in parallel, revealing structure across multiple temporal scales without explicit semantic tokens or multi-stage pipelines. At inference, future prediction heads are retained for look-ahead and aggregation. AuriStream-MTP achieves state-of-the-art performance across diverse speech tasks (SUPERB), demonstrates strong linguistic knowledge on lexical and syntactic tests (sWUGGY, sBLIMP), and produces more fluent and coherent speech generation than prior textless systems (GSLM), validated through automatic measures and human preferences. Our results demonstrate that multi-horizon prediction provides a simple, scalable, and universal objective for learning rich speech representations that support speech understanding and generation. We will release the code and weights upon acceptance.

We provide speech continuations generated by our model (AuriStream-MTP) to showcase its generation capabilities. We present multiple non-cherrypicked seeds of continuations over the standard LibriSpeech test set, as well as custom stimuli. AuriStream-MTP generates diverse and coherent continuations, demonstrating its ability to percieve, predict, and generate speech effectively.
